# Crypto Intelligence Briefing - 2026-04-23 17:31

## Crypto Intelligence Update - April 11, 2026

**Executive Briefing:**

The crypto landscape is currently defined by a significant shift towards institutional adoption of Bitcoin, driven by factors like diversification strategies, long-term growth potential, and increasing regulatory clarity. Spot Bitcoin ETFs have seen cumulative inflows nearing $58 billion, with over 2,000 US advisory firms now allocating to crypto ETFs – a dramatic increase from pre-2024 levels. This institutional interest is further bolstered by recent political developments, notably a $1.38 billion daily inflow following Trump’s election victory, signaling confidence in a potentially more favorable regulatory environment. The operational infrastructure supporting this adoption is maturing, with established access paths (ETFs, CME) and developing custody/prime services.

Alongside Bitcoin’s strengthening position, Ethereum is experiencing a surge in activity centered around restaking. This narrative has moved from speculative fringe to mainstream DeFi, transforming staked ETH into a multi-utility asset. Layer 2 scaling solutions, particularly Polygon, are powering this growth, with over 60 active rollups now processing the majority of Ethereum ecosystem transactions. The focus is shifting towards interoperability, evidenced by increasing activity in cross-chain bridges and the development of omnichain solutions for stablecoin transfers. However, capital fragmentation remains a key challenge, with liquidity siloed within individual blockchain ecosystems.

Crucially, the passage of the Clarity Act remains a pivotal point. While the bill aims to provide much-needed regulatory clarity for the US crypto industry, its progress is threatened by a tight Senate calendar and debate surrounding stablecoin yields. Failure to pass the Act could stifle innovation and potentially drive activity offshore, impacting the US’s position in the digital financial era. The outcome of the Clarity Act will significantly influence the trajectory of both BTC and ETH, as well as the broader crypto market.
