# Tax Intelligence Briefing - 2026-04-25 04:01

## Executive Briefing:

The global tax landscape is undergoing significant shifts, primarily driven by the OECD’s Pillar Two initiative aiming for a 15% global minimum corporate tax rate. Implementation is progressing, with approximately 140 countries adopting the framework. This will impact multinational enterprises (MNEs) with a “significant economic footprint” by potentially increasing their tax burden, particularly those currently benefiting from lower rates in jurisdictions like Ireland. The US is poised to play a key role, potentially applying the difference between the global minimum and lower local rates to its domestic tax base.

Concurrently, a resurgence of protectionist trade policies, particularly through tariffs, is emerging. The US is imposing and considering reciprocal tariffs on various countries, including China and the UK, with potential implications for global trade flows and inflation. The legality of these tariffs under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) is being challenged in court, adding uncertainty to the situation. The potential reversal of Trump-era tariffs could lead to significant revenue shifts and impact consumer prices.

Singapore remains a competitive jurisdiction with a headline corporate tax rate of 17%, but offers extensive exemptions and incentives. Dubai is also positioning itself as a business-friendly location with a 9% rate and 0% rates in Free Zones. These jurisdictions are likely to continue attracting investment as companies navigate the changing global tax environment. Austria is also adjusting its tax structure, reducing its top income tax rate.
